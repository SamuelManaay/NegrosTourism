About Negros Oriental

A tropical paradise with rich culture and breathtaking landscapes

Geography

Negros Oriental occupies the southeastern portion of Negros Island, spanning 5,402 square kilometers of mountainous terrain and coastal beauty. The province is renowned for its stunning natural landscapes and rich biodiversity.

Tourism

Discover hidden gems like the Twin Lakes in Sibulan and the spectacular Mabinay Caves. Experience vibrant festivals like Buglasan and Yag-yag, and enjoy national events including PBA games and the colorful Kitefest.

Infrastructure

The province boasts modern facilities including the Lamberto Macias Sports Center and Lorenzo Teves Memorial Aquacenter. Our healthcare network serves the region with hospitals, diagnostic centers, and rehabilitation facilities.

Social Services

We prioritize child and women's welfare through UNICEF partnerships. Our Tamlang resettlement project provides comprehensive support with education, health, and livelihood facilities for communities in need.

Economic Development

Through our progressive Investment Code, we welcome local and international businesses. The Investment Promotions Center actively supports economic growth and development opportunities throughout the province.

Environment

We're committed to sustainability through clean and green initiatives and wildlife conservation. Our weekly employee training programs ensure our team is equipped to protect Negros Oriental's natural treasures.
